## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are financial advisors, operations leads, and firm principals working inside a client-facing advisory practice. They use Drift during busy operating hours to prepare for meetings, review risk, coordinate follow-up, and prove that work is grounded in actual firm data.

### Brand Personality
Calm, exacting, inevitable. The product should feel trustworthy, operationally serious, and premium without slipping into generic "AI dashboard" cues.

### Aesthetic Direction
Default to a dark workspace optimized for long sessions with dense information. The visual tone should feel like expensive infrastructure for relationship-driven financial firms: restrained contrast, precise typography, tinted neutrals, and clear state signaling. Avoid playful startup gradients, purple AI branding, and overly decorative cards.

### Design Principles
1. Make the meeting-prep-to-follow-up loop feel like the center of the product.
2. Organize the app around advisor outcomes, not internal feature buckets.
3. Show confidence, freshness, and review state anywhere the product makes a recommendation.
4. Make household context feel rich, connected, and operational instead of CRM-like.
5. Use visual restraint so the product feels durable, premium, and easy to trust.
